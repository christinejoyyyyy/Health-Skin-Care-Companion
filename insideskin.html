Melasma

Melasma is a skin condition characterized by brown or blue-gray patches or freckle-like spots. It's often called the “mask of pregnancy.”

Melasma happens because of overproduction of the cells that make the color of your skin. It is common, harmless and some treatments may help. to join the fun.

Eczema is related to a gene variation that affects the skin's ability to provide this protection.

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a skin disorder that causes skin cells to multiply up to 10 times faster than normal. This makes the skin build up into bumpy red patches covered with white scales.

They can grow anywhere, but most appear on the scalp, elbows, knees, and lower back.

Acne

Acne is a skin condition that occurs when your hair follicles become plugged with oil and dead skin cells. It causes whiteheads, blackheads or pimples.

Rosacea

Rosacea is a common skin condition that causes redness and visible blood vessels in your face. It may also produce small, red, pus-filled bumps.
